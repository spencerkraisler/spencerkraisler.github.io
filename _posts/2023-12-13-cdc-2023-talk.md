I presented this work at the **62nd IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC 2023)** in Singapore.

### What problem was this about?
Distributed systems often need global geometric quantities (such as manifold averages) while only having local communication.

### What was the main contribution?
We developed distributed methods to compute geometric quantities with provable behavior, combining Riemannian geometry with consensus-style updates.

### Why it matters
These tools are useful for decentralized estimation and coordination in multi-agent systems with nonlinear state spaces.
